Positioning

Built for engineers, not lawyers

"The only GDPR compliance checker that asks whether your Elasticsearch index has a deletion hook wired to your primary store — not whether you have a privacy policy."
🧱

Policy questions tell you nothing

Every existing GDPR tool asks about consent mechanisms, lawful basis, and data subject forms. None of them ask whether your Kafka topics use field-level encryption with key deletion, or whether your reconciliation job catches orphaned directories.

📊

Binary pass/fail isn't a roadmap

A result of "partial compliance" tells your team nothing about where to focus effort. The 4-level maturity model produces a prioritized remediation roadmap, not a verdict — with effort estimates and direct links to the open source component that fixes each gap.

🔐

Reports without signatures are promises

A self-assessment that can be quietly revised is worthless to an auditor. Each assessment produces a timestamped, HMAC-SHA256 signed artifact. A DPA or enterprise customer can independently verify the artifact hasn't been modified since it was issued.

📈

Re-assessment tracks progress

The batch runner integrates into CI/CD. Run the assessment on every deploy. Each artifact is independently dated — so you can demonstrate to regulators that your posture improved from Foundational in Q1 to Advanced in Q3 with verifiable evidence.

Scoring model

4-level maturity per control

Controls are scored on a maturity scale rather than binary pass/fail. A regulator seeing "Implemented but not Tested" treats it more credibly than an unexplained "partial."

0
None

Control does not exist. Gap is open and unaddressed.

1
Documented

Policy or design exists on paper. Not yet in production code.

2
Implemented

Code or infrastructure change is live in production.

3
Tested

Automated tests verify the control works. CI runs on every deploy.

4
Monitored

Continuous monitoring with alerting. Reconciliation jobs catch regressions.

Overall score = weighted average across all controls, expressed as a percentage and a maturity tier: Foundational (<25%) · Developing (<50%) · Advanced (<75%) · Exemplary (≥75%)

14 technical control sections

Each section targets a specific layer of the deletion pipeline — from primary storage and Kafka topics to ML model governance and cryptographic erasure.

Medium
§4

Message Queues & Event Streams

Kafka compacted topics, field-level encryption with key deletion, retention window documentation for personal data in event streams.

Critical
§5

Backup & Archive Storage

Residual retention window, backup purge process, legal hold interaction, communication to data subjects about backup retention.

High
§6

Logs & Observability Data

PII inventory in logs (IP addresses, user IDs, request params), anonymization at ingestion, retention windows, record-level deletion capability.

Medium
§7

Third-Party Processors

Deletion propagation to CRM, analytics, error tracking, support tools. Contractual coverage and verification mechanism per processor.

High
§8

Machine Learning Models

Training data inventory, unlearning / retraining exclusion, honest disclosure of the limits of file-level deletion vs model influence.

Critical
§9

Deletion Receipt Issuance

Cryptographically signed receipts at deletion time, canonical JSON signing, signing key rotation, key management maturity.

Critical
§10

Audit Log Integrity

Append-only audit log, hash chaining, access control separation from operational DB, on-demand chain verification.

Critical
§11

Legal Hold Procedures

Hold suspension mechanism, hold notification issuance, post-hold deletion re-queue, data subject communication procedures.

High
§12

Federated & Multi-Node Systems

Distributed deletion coordination, per-node acknowledgment before receipt, unreachable-node reconciliation mechanism.

Medium
§13

Cryptographic Erasure

Per-user AES-256-GCM encryption with key deletion for Kafka and backups, KMS separation, key deletion audit, disclosure to data subjects.

Critical
§14

Deletion Testing & Verification

Integration tests against real infrastructure (not mocks), CI gating, scheduled audit jobs, out-of-band verification scripts.

assessment_id

UUID4 prefix — every assessment is unique and independently referenceable. Re-run after remediation and both artifacts coexist with different IDs.

organization_id

An anonymized hash or identifier you supply. No organization name is stored server-side.

section_scores

Per-section percentage score, weighted by your selected vertical. Sections at 0% are critical gaps regardless of overall tier.

signature

HMAC-SHA256 over json.dumps(payload, sort_keys=True). Anyone with the key can verify — no server needed.

critical_gaps

Sections scoring below 25% — where regulatory risk exposure is highest. These are the first items in the remediation roadmap.

Automate compliance gating

The batch runner reads a YAML config with pre-scored answers, runs the assessment headlessly, and exits 0 (pass) or 1 (fail) based on a configurable score threshold. Drop it into your pipeline.

The ecosystem

Assessment → remediation → re-assessment

The compliance checker is the entry point to the deleteceipt ecosystem. It is not just a diagnostic — it is a curriculum that walks an organization from zero to exemplary deletion compliance, with open source tools at every step.

1

Run the assessment → discover receipt gap (level 0)

The checker identifies that no deletion receipt is issued at deletion time. Critical gap in §9 — highest regulatory risk.

2

Remediation roadmap points to deleteceipt v0.1.0

pip install deleteceipt — integrate HMAC-SHA256 signed receipts into your deletion pipeline. Zero dependencies.

3

Re-run assessment → §9 moves from level 0 to level 2

Receipt issuance is now Implemented. New signed artifact is dated independently. Both artifacts coexist — auditors can compare them.

4

Add integration tests → level 2 to level 3

Wire deletion-audit-test into CI. Tests verify deletion across each real storage layer on every deploy.

5

Deploy reconciliation with monitoring → level 3 to level 4

deleteceipt-reconcile catches Type-A (no receipt) and Type-B (orphaned directory) discrepancies. Reconciliation runs on a schedule with alerting.

6

Export enterprise receipts → present to your own customers

deleteceipt-enterprise generates GDPR Article 30 receipts for vendor management evidence. Your compliance becomes your customers' compliance.
